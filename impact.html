The Impact of Insecurity

Cybersecurity is no longer a "tech issue"—it is a human rights issue. When digital systems fail, the consequences ripple through the real world, affecting everything from personal privacy to national infrastructure.

The Personal Cost: Identity Theft

Every year, millions of people have their lives turned upside down because of poor digital security. Identity theft occurs when a hacker gains access to your personal data (Social Security numbers, birth dates, and addresses) and uses it to open credit cards, take out loans, or even commit crimes in your name.

For a teenager, this can be devastating. If your identity is stolen at 15, you might find out when you turn 18 that your credit score is ruined, making it impossible to get a student loan or rent an apartment. This is why using unique passwords and MFA is so critical—it protects your future self.

The Societal Cost: Infrastructure and Privacy

Beyond personal loss, cyberattacks threaten the systems we rely on to live. Ransomware attacks have successfully shut down hospitals, preventing doctors from seeing patient records and forcing surgeries to be canceled. In some cases, hackers have targeted city power grids and water treatment plants.

Furthermore, the loss of privacy leads to a "Chilling Effect." If people feel they are always being watched or that their private messages could be leaked at any time, they stop speaking freely. Cybersecurity and encryption are the tools that preserve Freedom of Speech in the digital age. By securing your own data, you are contributing to a safer, more private internet for everyone.
