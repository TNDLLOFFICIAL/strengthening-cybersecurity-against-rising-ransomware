Engineering Mitigation Protocols

01. Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA)

The "Castle and Moat" defense is dead. ZTA operates on the principle of "Never Trust, Always Verify." Every request for access to the internal network—whether from a CEO or a server—must be re-authenticated and inspected in real-time. This prevents "Lateral Movement" if one account is compromised.

Micro-segmentation Continuous Auth Least Privilege

02. Immutable Storage & Air-Gapping

Ransomware now targets backups first. The engineering solution is "Immutability"—storage that is physically Write-Once-Read-Many (WORM). Once data is written to these drives, the hardware itself prevents it from being deleted or modified for a set period, even by a system administrator.

03. Agentic AI Firewalls

To fight autonomous malware, we use autonomous defense. AI Firewalls analyze network patterns in microseconds. If a user begins downloading data at an "impossible" speed or from an unusual location, the AI severs the connection instantly, long before a human admin could even receive the alert.

04. The 3-2-1-1 Rule

Engineering resilience requires 3 copies of data, on 2 different types of media, with 1 copy off-site, and 1 copy being completely offline (air-gapped) and immutable.
