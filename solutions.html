01. The Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA)

Traditional security relied on the "Castle and Moat" strategy. Modern security has moved to Zero Trust. The core philosophy is: "Never Trust, Always Verify."

The Three Pillars of Zero Trust

I. Identity Verification

Moving beyond passwords to biometric and hardware-based Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).

II. Least Privilege Access

Users are given only the minimum level of access required to do their jobs.

III. Micro-Segmentation

Breaking the network into small, isolated zones to contain potential attacks.

02. Advanced Network Hardening

Hardening is the process of securing a system by reducing its surface of vulnerability. This includes implementing End-to-End Encryption (E2EE) using TLS 1.3 protocols.
