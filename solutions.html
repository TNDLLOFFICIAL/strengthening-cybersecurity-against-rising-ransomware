Engineering Resilience: Modern Solutions

🛡️ 01. Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA)

The old "castle and moat" strategy failed. Zero Trust operates on the principle: "Never Trust, Always Verify."

  • Micro-segmentation: Breaking the network into tiny zones. If a hacker gets into one "room," the rest of the building stays locked.
  • Continuous Authentication: The system checks who you are every time you try to open a new file, not just when you log in.

💾 02. Immutable & Air-Gapped Backups

Hackers now try to delete your backups first. The engineering solution is Immutability.

Immutable Data is "Write Once, Read Many" (WORM). Even if a hacker has admin access, the physics of the storage prevents them from deleting or changing the data for a set period (e.g., 30 days).

🤖 03. AI-Driven XDR (Extended Detection)

To fight AI, we use AI. XDR systems monitor billions of data points across a company to find "Anomalous Behavior."

Example: If an employee usually downloads 10MB of data a day but suddenly starts downloading 50GB at 3:00 AM from an IP address in a different country, the AI automatically severs the connection in milliseconds.
