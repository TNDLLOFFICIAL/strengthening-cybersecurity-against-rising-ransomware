The Science of Encryption

How math keeps your private messages private.

What is Cryptography?

Cryptography is the study of hidden writing. In the digital world, it’s the process of turning readable data into a secret code. When you send a text, your phone uses a Cipher (a math formula) to scramble the words.

If a hacker steals that data while it's traveling through the air, all they see is random gibberish. They can't read it unless they have the specific Key to unlock it. This is how your bank info and passwords stay safe every day.

How the "Keys" Work

There are two main ways computers handle these secret keys:

  • Symmetric Encryption: This is like a physical diary with a lock. Both people have the exact same key to lock and unlock the message.
  • Asymmetric Encryption: This is more advanced. You have a "Public Key" that you give to everyone so they can lock messages for you, but you keep a "Private Key" that only you own to unlock them. Even if someone steals the lock, they can't open it without your private key.
