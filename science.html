Cryptographic Vector Analysis

01. Asymmetric Prime Factorization

Modern encryption relies on the mathematical complexity of the RSA algorithm. The security of the system is based on the fact that multiplying two large prime numbers is easy, but factoring the resulting product (the public key) is computationally unfeasible for classical computers.

02. Hybrid Encryption Schemes

Ransomware does not use RSA to encrypt large files because it is too slow. Instead, it uses a hybrid approach: AES-256 (Symmetric) to lock the actual data, and RSA (Asymmetric) to encrypt the AES key. This combines the speed of symmetric math with the security of asymmetric key exchange.

03. Behavioral Psychology (Cognitive Science)

The "Science" of the breach often targets the human element. Attackers utilize Cognitive Overload—sending multiple urgent, conflicting alerts to an administrator to force a rapid, irrational decision that bypasses standard security protocols.

04. Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC)

The 2026 intelligence landscape is shifting toward PQC. As quantum computers advance, algorithms like Shor’s threaten to solve the prime factorization problem in minutes, requiring a complete rewrite of global encryption standards.
