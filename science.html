Computational Complexity

The security of RSA-4096 is estimated at 2^128 bits of security, requiring more energy than the sun produces in its lifetime to crack via brute force.

The Quantum Threat

Shor's Algorithm allows a quantum computer to factor integers in polynomial time (O(log n)^3), rendering existing asymmetric encryption obsolete.

Lattice-Based Post-Quantum Logic

We are transitioning to "Lattice-Based" cryptography, which involves finding the Shortest Vector Problem (SVP) in a high-dimensional space. This problem is considered "Quantum Resistant."
