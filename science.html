The Science of Encryption & Human Behavior

01. Behavioral Science: Social Engineering

The "Science" of ransomware isn't just code; it's Cognitive Psychology. Hackers use "Amydgala Hijacking"—creating a state of panic so the victim bypasses their rational thinking.

  • Urgency: "Your account will be deleted in 10 minutes."
  • Authority: Mimicking a high-ranking official or government agency.
  • Fear: Threats of legal action or public embarrassment.

02. The Mathematics of Cryptography

Ransomware uses Asymmetric Encryption. This relies on the mathematical difficulty of factoring large prime numbers.

RSA Algorithm Logic

1. Choose two large prime numbers, p and q.

2. Calculate n = p * q.

3. The difficulty for a defender is that while multiplying p and q is easy, finding p and q from a massive n is mathematically impossible for modern computers.

Computational Complexity

To "brute force" a 2048-bit RSA key, it would take a standard supercomputer approximately 300 trillion years. This is why you can't just "guess" the password once your files are locked.

03. The Quantum Leap (2026 Perspective)

The new "unsolved" math problem is Shor's Algorithm. If a functional Quantum Computer is built, it could solve the prime factorization problem in minutes, making all current encryption useless.
