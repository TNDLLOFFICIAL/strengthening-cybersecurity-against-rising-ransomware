Cryptographic Vulnerability Analysis

01. The Math of Prime Factorization

Ransomware utilizes Asymmetric Encryption, primarily RSA-4096. The mathematical "unsolved" problem here is the Prime Factorization Problem. While it is easy to multiply two enormous prime numbers (P and Q), it is mathematically impossible for current supercomputers to reverse the process and find the factors from the product (N) within a human lifetime.

// CRYPTOGRAPHIC CHALLENGE
Encryption: C = M^e mod n
Decryption: M = C^d mod n
Required compute time for brute-force: > 100 Trillion Years.

02. Symmetric Speed: AES-256

Attackers do not use Asymmetric encryption for files (too slow). They use Symmetric AES-256 to lock the data. The "Science" lies in the 14 rounds of substitution and permutation. The RSA key is only used to lock the tiny AES key, creating a nearly unbreakable "digital vault" in milliseconds.

03. Behavioral Science: Amygdala Hijacking

Cybersecurity is also a Social Science. Attackers use "Psychological Priming" to trigger the human Amygdala (the fear center of the brain). By creating high-stress scenarios (e.g., "Your account will be deleted in 4 minutes"), they force victims to bypass logical reasoning and grant administrative access.

04. Post-Quantum Math (PQC)

The next frontier is "Lattice-based Cryptography." We are analyzing mathematical problems that even quantum computers (using Shor's Algorithm) cannot solve efficiently. In 2026, transitioning to PQC is the only way to ensure long-term data survival.
