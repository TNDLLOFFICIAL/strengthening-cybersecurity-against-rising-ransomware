The Modern Threat Landscape

Cybersecurity threats are no longer just "viruses" that slow down your computer. They have evolved into highly sophisticated, multi-stage attacks designed for financial gain, espionage, and total system destruction.

1. Social Engineering & Phishing

Social engineering is the psychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information. Phishing is the most common form, where attackers send fraudulent communications that appear to come from a reputable source.

  • Spear Phishing: A highly targeted attack aimed at a specific individual or organization.
  • Whaling: A form of phishing aimed at high-level executives like CEOs.

2. Ransomware: Digital Kidnapping

Ransomware is a type of malware that threatens to publish the victim's personal data or perpetually block access to it unless a ransom is paid. Modern ransomware often uses "Double Extortion," where they not only lock your files but also steal them to leak online if you don't pay up.

3. Zero-Day Vulnerabilities

A zero-day is a flaw in software or hardware that is unknown to the party responsible for patching or fixing the flaw. Because the developers haven't heard of it yet, they have "zero days" to fix it before it can be exploited by hackers.
